About inScript

inScript is a browser-based Bible-study tool created by the Digital Bible Society. It is free, multilingual, and requires no installation. It runs online or fully offline from a thumb drive, DVD, or SD card. It was built especially to serve readers in restricted-access regions.

Its clean reading view sits on top of powerful study tools: full-text search, side-by-side translation comparison, searchable concordances, smart-scrolling audio Bibles, maps, footnotes, commentaries, and original-language word studies. We invite you to share inScript freely and to help it grow. Contact us to get involved.

Getting Started

inScript is designed to be simple to read and powerful to study. Here is how to get the most out of it:

  1. Open a Bible. Use the menu or search box to choose any translation. It opens in a reading panel you can scroll freely.
  2. Compare translations. Open a second (or third) panel to read versions side by side, verse for verse.
  3. Search the Scriptures. Type a word, phrase, or reference into the search box to find passages across your open Bibles.
  4. Listen along. Many Bibles include smart-scrolling audio. Press play and the text follows the narration.
  5. Dig deeper. Tap footnotes, cross-references, maps, commentaries, and original-language word studies wherever they appear.
  6. Check copyright. Tap the “i” icon in any Bible’s header to see its license and usage terms.
  7. Adjust the view. Open the gear (settings) menu to change text size, layout, and interface language.
  8. Use it anywhere. inScript needs no install and runs offline from DBS Treasures libraries, so it keeps working where connections do not.

Frequently Asked Questions

Bibles

I can’t find the Bible I want. What now?

New translations are added all the time, so check back soon. If there is a specific Bible you would like to see, let us know, and include its copyright details if you can. As a non-profit ministry, we also welcome partners who can help sponsor titles that carry royalty or licensing costs.

I found an error in a Bible. How do I report it?

Please tell us through our feedback page and be as specific as possible: the translation, the reference, and what looks wrong. We will look into it as quickly as we can.

Why are some Bibles incomplete?

Translating a full Bible takes years, sometimes decades. So that readers don’t have to wait, we publish completed books as soon as the translators release them, then replace those portions as the work is finished. If your organization has a translation in progress, we would love to partner with you.

Can I copy or freely distribute these Bibles?

It depends on the translation. Some are public domain or Creative Commons licensed; many others carry copyright and distribution limits set by their publishers. Tap the “i” icon in a Bible’s header to see its specific terms, and contact the publisher directly for anything beyond that.

Using inScript

inScript won’t load, or runs slowly. What can I do?

First, try a hard reset of the page. This reloads inScript fresh and bypasses the old copy your browser has cached, which clears up most loading problems:

  • Windows or Linux PC: press Ctrl + Shift + R (or Ctrl + F5).
  • Mac: press Cmd + Shift + R.

If that does not help, clear your browser’s cache or reset the browser to its defaults. On a weak connection, please be patient or switch networks. Note that a few not-yet-indexed Bibles can make searches slower. inScript performs best in Chrome or Firefox.

Can I print Bible text?

Yes. To print what’s on screen, use your browser’s print option. For more than one screen’s worth, select and copy the text into another program (such as a word processor) and print from there.

Is there a mobile app?

inScript runs in the web browser on virtually any device today. Dedicated apps are in development, so check back for release news.

How can I support inScript?

Pray that inScript would carry the Scriptures into every language. Share it with others, link to it from your site, and tell friends about the work of the Digital Bible Society. You can also make a donation toward its continued development.

Community

inScript is a free and fully open-source project, built in the open and shaped by the people who use it. We warmly welcome your feedback, suggestions, and bug reports: tell us what works well, what is missing, and what you would love to see next.

Run inScript on your own site

Churches, ministries, and developers are free to download and install inScript from GitHub and host it on their own website, server, or offline media. Put it to work in your own context, and partner with us actively to make it better for everyone.

Join the conversation

Because inScript belongs to the community, its forum lives on GitHub. Join us there to ask questions, request features, and report issues. Then fork the code and open a pull request to contribute directly.
